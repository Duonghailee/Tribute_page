Professor. Louis Pasteur

Founder of bacteriology

Pasteur experimenting in his laboratory

Here is a time line of Professor.Louis Pasteur's life:

  • 1822 - Born in Dole, France
  • 1847 - goes to Ecole Normal Colledge, France
  • 1849 - Gets married and had 6 children
  • 1854 - Becomes professor of Science
  • 1855 - Finds bacteria in the drink
  • 1856 - Returns to Ecole Normal
  • 1864 - Studies where the bacteria came from
  • 1864 - Proves that germs were the cause of illness - The Germ Theory
  • 1865 - Saves the silkworms
  • 1879 - Discoveres vaccinations
  • 1885 - A vaccine against rabies
  • 1888 - The Pasteur Institute in Paris was found
  • 1895 - is honored The Leeuwenhoek medal, the highest honor in microbiology
  • 1895 - Dies at the age 72 in Marnes-la-Coquette, France
"Absolute faith in God and in Eternity, and a conviction that the power for good given to us in this world will be continued beyond it, were feelings which pervaded his whole life; the virtues of the gospel had ever been present to him. Full of respect for the form of religion which had been that of his forefathers, he came simply to it and naturally for spiritual help in these last weeks of his life"

-Vallery-Radot 1911, vol. 2, p. 240
